Weather Forecasting Definition

Weather forecasting uses tools, observational data, science, and technology to predict the weather hours, days, and months in advance for a given location. Before computer models, weather forecasting was done by observation. The first computer-generated weather forecast was made in 1955. Weather forecasting is used for knowing our daily weather and also used for long-term forecasting. Forecasting the weather involves recording the ongoing measurements of temperature, pressure, precipitation, wind speed, and the amount of cloud cover, and giving those current readings and reports to the public.

What Is the Weather Forecast?

Weather is always happening. When outside, it is what is all around you. It is how the atmosphere is behaving with regard to temperature, pressure, wind speed, and precipitation. This is called the weather report, or current weather conditions. It might be sunny, calm, and warm outside, but in a few hours, it can change. Clouds can roll in bringing with them cooler temperatures and rain. Predicting what the weather will be in three hours from now, to twenty-four hours, to next week is what weather forecasting aims to accomplish.

What Is Weather Forecasting?

Should you wear a raincoat or a sweater today? Should you go to the office or work from home? These are all everyday questions we might only be able to answer with the help of a weather forecast. When dangerous weather conditions like hurricanes or snowstorms arrive, our choices about what we should or shouldn't do can often mean the difference between life and death.

Weather forecasting is the prediction of what the atmosphere will be like in a particular place by using technology and scientific knowledge to make weather observations. In other words, it's a way of predicting things like cloud cover, rain, snow, wind speed, and temperature before they happen.

Barometers

Barometers are instruments that measure atmospheric pressure. The air pressure is the weight of air above a given level. It is an important tool in forecasting because air pressure and air temperature are related. If one changes, it affects the other. Warm air is usually associated with higher pressure readings and cold air is associated with lower pressure readings. The temperature difference creates a horizontal difference in pressure, which causes the air to move from areas of high pressure to low pressure. Think of a hot and humid summer afternoon, the atmosphere feels almost tense with uncertainty. An approaching cold front, the cooler air, will eventually meet up with the warm air to balance out the atmosphere. Simply put, this movement of air wanting balance is our weather.

Radar

Radar, which stands for Radio Detection and Ranging, is one of the most important tools in weather forecasting. It works by emitting short powerful microwave pulses, and when that wave hits a target, be it rain, cloud, or even an insect, part of the wave bounces back. That return is then amplified and displayed on a screen. The brightness of the return echo is related to the amount of rain inside a cloud. So a radar detects the location and intensity of the precipitation. Red and purple colors on the radar indicate strong returns, and therefore intense precipitation. Radars also can aid in the detection of tornadoes, as how the returns present on the map can indicate rotation.

What is an example of weather forecasting?

Severe or hazardous weather forecasting is implemented up to seven days in advance by the storm prediction center. It predicts the probability and potential for severe weather events such as thunderstorms, flooding, hurricanes, tornadoes, and fires.

What is weather forecasting and its types?

Weather forecasting is predicting atmospheric conditions of the atmosphere for a given location. There are four types of forecasts: short-range (12-48 hours), medium-range (3-7 days), long-range (8 days +), and hazardous/severe weather forecasts.
